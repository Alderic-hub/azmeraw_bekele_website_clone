  • Black Cumin

Nigella sativa, the scientific name for black cumin seed, is a tiny black seed that is extracted from the same-named flowering plant. It's also frequently called "black."

Product Detail

Appearance:

  • The tiny, somewhat curved black cumin seeds are dark in hue.

  • They resemble tiny black sesame seeds due to their distinctive form.

Flavor and aroma:

  • A strong, spicy, and slightly bitter flavor characterizes black cumin seeds. 

  • They also smell strongly, with descriptions ranging from earthy to oregano or black pepper.

Culinary Uses:In many different cuisines, especially Middle Eastern, Indian, and North African dishes, black cumin seeds are used as a spice and flavoring.Before using them in dishes, they are frequently lightly toasted or dry-roasted to bring out the flavor.Curries, soups, stews, bread, pickles, and spice blends all contain black cumin seeds.They can also be added to herbal teas or sprinkled over salads.Health Benefits:For a very long time, black cumin seeds were prized for their possible health advantages.They are abundant in various bioactive substances, important fatty acids, and antioxidants.Black cumin seeds are thought to possess immune-stimulating, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory qualities.It's crucial to remember, nevertheless, that additional scientific study is required in order to completely comprehend and confirm these possible advantages.
